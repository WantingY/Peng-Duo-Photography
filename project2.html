Occupy the Wall Street


Occupy the Wall Street


In our history, with one generation disappeared on the stage, there is not only the time been taken away with them, but also the memories about all the events happened in those days. After one hundred years, the only way to know those events is acquiring from those historical documents.


However, all these documents, including books, images and videos, were dominated by their creators’ subjective understanding of what’s going on at that time. In this way, when all the creators had the same point of focus on an event, the other part of the event, which no one really cares, are been erased in the history with nothing left.


Unfortunately, in most events happened in the history, most of the historical documents creators focus on the same part of it, fire and death in a war, pain and sadness in a disaster, power and passion in an election, conflict and chaos in a movement.


Occupy the Wall Street, is just another movement in the history our generation are being through. I am just trying to use my camera with my subjective point of view, documenting those moments and subjects seem not related to the story that the events should have in the “objective history”.


The diversity aspects of all the events deserve to be recorded in the history, even the quietness and peacefulness no different from our daily life. The main story can never use as a substitute of the whole story.
